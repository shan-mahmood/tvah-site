At Tustin Village Animal Hospital, we offer a full range of veterinary surgical services to meet your pet's needs. ## Types of Surgeries We Perform

- **Spay & neuter surgery** — Prevents unwanted pregnancies and reduces risk of certain cancers and behavioral issues, contributing to longer, healthier pet lives.
- **Dental surgery** — Covers removal of infected, fractured, or diseased teeth to prevent pain, infections, and long-term health complications.
- **Soft tissue surgery** — Includes mass removals, foreign body extractions, and reconstructive procedures for various medical conditions.
- **Orthopedic surgery** — Advanced procedures including TPLO surgery for knee stabilization and limb amputations to restore mobility and reduce pain.
- **Emergency surgery** — Critical situations requiring immediate intervention, from Cesarean sections to trauma repair.

## FAQs

**Q: How do I know if my pet needs surgery?**
A: Our veterinarians conduct thorough examinations and discuss all available treatment options, recommending surgery only when necessary for your pet's health and well-being.

**Q: What should I do to prepare my pet for surgery?**
A: Ensure a pre-surgery checkup, follow fasting instructions (no food for 12 hours, no water for 6 hours), inform the vet of any medications, keep your pet calm, arrive on time, and discuss post-surgery care.

**Q: What is the recovery process like?**
A: Recovery times vary by surgery type, typically involving rest, limited activity, and pain management discussed with your veterinarian.

**Q: Is anesthesia safe for my pet?**
A: We use the latest anesthesia protocols and monitoring equipment, with careful pre-anesthesia patient evaluation.

**Q: How can I help my pet heal after surgery?**
A: Follow post-operative instructions, administer prescribed medications, and maintain a calm, comfortable environment.
