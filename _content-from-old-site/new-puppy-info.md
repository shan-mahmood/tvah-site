# New Pup Essentials: Start Off on the Right Paw

Bringing home a new puppy is an exciting adventure! Tustin Village Animal Hospital provides essential information on puppy care, vaccinations, nutrition, and tips to ensure your pup grows up happy and healthy.

## Puppy's Health Journey: Vaccination Schedule

Ensuring your new puppy stays healthy and protected is one of the most important aspects of pet ownership. Vaccinations safeguard puppies from various contagious diseases. Your puppy is not considered immune until 7-10 days after the vaccine series is complete, so avoid taking them to public areas during this period.

### DHPPV / DAPPV Vaccination

The parvovirus vaccine is a 4-in-1 vaccination covering Distemper, Hepatitis (Adenovirus), Parainfluenza, and Parvovirus. The first dose is administered as early as six weeks old, then given at 2-4 week intervals until at least 16 weeks old (totaling 3-4 doses). A booster is given one year after the final dose, then every three years.

### What Is Canine Distemper?

Canine distemper is an extremely contagious viral disease closely related to measles that spreads through the air. The virus attacks the gastrointestinal, respiratory, urogenital, and nervous systems. There is no cure, though some dogs recover with supportive treatment.

Symptoms include high fever, runny nose, eye discharge, lethargy, loss of appetite, coughing, vomiting, diarrhea, seizures, and paralysis. Some dogs experience thickened or enlarged footpads.

### What Is Adenovirus & Hepatitis?

Canine adenovirus type 1 causes hepatitis, resulting in swelling and cell damage in the liver, potentially causing hemorrhage and death. The virus spreads through feces and urine of infected dogs.

Symptoms include abdominal pain, abdominal distension, loss of appetite, pale color, lethargy, fever, and tonsillitis. Fluid swelling in corneas often causes a "blue eye" appearance. Death within one to two days is common in severe cases, though survivors develop immunity.

Canine adenovirus type 2 is related to hepatitis and causes kennel cough. Once vaccinated, severity is limited and death is unlikely.

Symptoms include a hacking cough one week after exposure, airway inflammation, white foamy discharge after coughing, pink eye, and nasal inflammation.

### What Is Parainfluenza?

Parainfluenza (canine influenza) is highly contagious, with symptoms including dry cough, fever, wheezing, difficulty breathing, runny nose, sneezing, pneumonia, reduced appetite, lethargy, and eye inflammation. Most dogs recover independently, but immediate antibiotic and antiviral treatment is recommended due to contagiousness.

### What Is Parvovirus?

Canine parvovirus is extremely contagious, contracted through feces of infected dogs and living in the environment for up to 3 days. Parvo often kills young puppies; around 91% of untreated cases result in death. The vaccine is the only prevention.

Dogs show symptoms within three to ten days. Common symptoms include secondary infections, dehydration, lethargy, diarrhea, vomiting, endotoxemia, shock, and death. Dogs shed the virus in feces after recovery, and the vaccine takes up to two weeks to take full effect.

### Rabies Vaccination

The rabies vaccine is given at 16 weeks of age. Rabies is a viral disease carried by many mammals and one of the few zoonotic diseases humans can catch from dogs. It spreads through bites from infected mammals and causes acute encephalitis, eventually infecting the entire nervous system and causing death. If treated before symptoms appear, rabies can be stopped; once symptoms appear, it becomes fatal. It can take 2-12 weeks or longer to present.

## Information About Non-Core Puppy Vaccinations

### What Is Bordetella?

Bordetella (kennel cough) is caused by bacteria and is spread through airborne contaminants. It spreads through exposure to infected dogs or contaminated food/water bowls and cages.

Symptoms include fever, sneezing, nasal discharge, loss of appetite, and depression. The dog should receive antibiotics and cough suppressant. Untreated bordetella can lead to pneumonia and secondary bacterial infection.

### What Is Canine Influenza?

Canine influenza (CIV) is highly contagious and spreads through direct contact, nasal secretions (barking, coughing, sneezing), contaminated objects (kennel surfaces, bowls, collars, leashes), and by people moving between infected and uninfected dogs.

It can occur year-round and present as lethargy, discharge, fever, dehydration, inappetence, vomiting, or diarrhea, potentially progressing to pneumonia. CIV can be life-threatening in immunocompromised patients, very young, and very old dogs.

### What Is Leptospirosis?

Leptospirosis is a bacterial infection caused by Leptospira that affects both canines and humans and can result in death.

Dogs become infected by consuming urine-contaminated water or contacting infected urine. Early symptoms include fever, vomiting, depression, loss of appetite, generalized pain, and conjunctivitis. Later symptoms include temperature drop, increased thirst, urine color change, jaundice, frequent urination, dehydration, difficulty breathing, muscular tremors, vomiting, and bloody feces.

Antibiotics can shorten disease length and reduce organ damage if caught early. Severe cases may require kidney filtration and blood transfusion. About 10% of cases result in death from secondary complications.

### What Is Lyme Disease?

Lyme disease spreads through tick bites. Symptoms don't always appear; some dogs show swollen lymph nodes or lameness. Untreated Lyme disease can cause extreme inflammation in the nervous system, heart, and kidneys, potentially leading to death.

Vets test via blood samples. Positive dogs receive early Doxycycline treatment. Advanced stages may require longer antibiotic treatment and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs.

### Side Effects And Risks Associated With Dog Vaccinations

The benefits of vaccinations far outweigh any risks. Adverse reactions are rare. However, vaccinations can cause some side effects, and monitoring your puppy after vaccination is recommended.

Potential symptoms include:
- Fever
- Sluggishness
- Loss of appetite
- Facial or paw swelling and/or hives
- Vomiting
- Diarrhea
- Pain or swelling around the injection site
- Collapse, difficulty breathing, and seizures (anaphylactic shock)

If your dog shows any symptoms after vaccination, contact your veterinary office or an emergency clinic.

## Additional Information

### Heartworm Prevention

Heartworms are parasites lodging in the heart and lung vessels, spread by mosquitoes. Start preventative tablets at 8 weeks and continue for the dog's lifetime. Annual testing is recommended.

### Fleas & Tick Prevention

Products carried include:

- **Revolution Plus Puppy:** Topical insecticide applied monthly; kills fleas, ticks, and heartworms
- **Simparica:** Oral insecticide given monthly; kills fleas and ticks within 12 hours
- **Bravecto:** Oral insecticide given every 3 months; kills fleas and ticks within 12 hours (only for dogs >6 months)

### The Fecal Exam

Many intestinal parasites puppies can carry exist. Bring a fecal sample for special testing evaluation. Some parasites are zoonotic (transmissible to humans), making this important for your pet's health and family protection.

### Diet

From weaning until 1 year of age, feed main brand puppy food such as Royal Canin, Science Diet, Iams, Eukanuba, Nutro, Pedigree, or Purina. Train puppies to be meal-fed rather than free-fed to prevent problems later in life.

**Small Breed Puppies:** Feed frequent small meals until about 16 weeks old to prevent low blood sugar problems.

**Large Breed Puppies:** Such as Great Danes, Dobermans, Golden Retrievers, Rottweilers, and Labradors, feed large-breed designated puppy food. This special food has fewer calories per cup than regular puppy food and scientifically reduces orthopedic illness chances.

### Spay & Neuter

Spay female puppies at 6-8 months old; neuter males around 1 year of age. Spaying before the first heat critically reduces mammary cancer development chances in adult females. Neutering reduces prostate inflammation and testicular cancer problems in adult males. Multiple studies are currently evaluating optimal spay/neuter timing; discuss timing with your veterinarian.

### Puppy Training

Training courses are recommended for every puppy and owner, typically starting after 12 weeks of age, once two vaccination sets have been given.
